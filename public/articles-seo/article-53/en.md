# How to Use Your CPF for a Complete Career Change: Complete Guide 2026

The **CPF (Personal Training Account)** is a valuable tool to fund your career change. But how to use it effectively? What training is eligible? This complete guide answers all your questions about using the CPF for a complete career change.

## What is the CPF?

The **Personal Training Account (CPF)** is a system that allows any working person to acquire training rights throughout their professional life.

### How the CPF Works

**Annual Credit**:
- **€500 per year** for employees with lower qualifications
- **€800 per year** for employees with higher qualifications
- **Cap**: €5,000 to €8,000 depending on qualification level

**Who Can Use It?**
- All active workers (employees, job seekers, self-employed)
- From age 16
- Until retirement

## Why Use the CPF for a Career Change?

### CPF Advantages

**1. Automatic Funding**: No need to ask your employer for permission
**2. Certified Training**: Access to recognized training
**3. Wide Choice**: Thousands of training options available
**4. Possible Supplements**: Other funding can complement if CPF isn't enough

## What CPF Training for a Career Change?

### Types of Eligible Training

**1. Professional Certifications**: Recognized by industry bodies
**2. Professional Titles**: RNCP titles (National Directory of Professional Certifications)
**3. Professional Qualification Certificates (CQP)**: Sector-specific certifications
**4. Short Specialized Training**: Specific job skills

### Growing Sectors for Career Change with CPF

**Digital and Technology**: Web development, Cybersecurity, Data Science, Digital marketing

**Commerce and Sales**: Sales techniques, E-commerce, Customer relations

**Personal Services**: Home care, Childcare, Wellness and health

**Environment**: Renewable energy, CSR, Sustainable development

## Supplementing CPF Funding

### If Your CPF Isn't Enough

**Options**:
1. **Employment Agency**: Individual Training Assistance
2. **OPCO (Skills Operators)**: Funding based on your sector
3. **Regions**: Regional training assistance
4. **Employer**: If employed, your employer can co-fund
5. **Bank Loans**: Training loans
