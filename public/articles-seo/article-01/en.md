# How to Become a Web Developer in 2025: Complete Guide to Training, Salary and Skills

The **web developer** profession continues to attract many candidates in 2025. With the growing digitalization of companies and the explosion of web application needs, this sector offers promising career opportunities. Whether you're a student, career changer, or simply curious, this complete guide will help you in your project to become a web developer.

## Why Become a Web Developer in 2025?

The digital sector is experiencing exceptional growth. According to recent studies, **more than 80,000 developer positions** will be available in France by 2026. This demand is explained by several factors:

- **Digital transformation**: All companies, from startups to large corporations, need websites and applications
- **E-commerce growth**: Online sales continue to grow, requiring performant platforms
- **Remote work**: Collaborative tools and web applications have become essential
- **Technological innovation**: New technologies (AI, blockchain, IoT) create new needs

The web developer profession also offers great **flexibility**: possibility to work as a freelancer, remotely, or in a company. Salaries are attractive and career prospects are numerous.

## What is a Web Developer?

A **web developer** is a professional who designs, develops, and maintains websites and web applications. They work with different programming languages to create user interfaces (frontend) and server functionalities (backend).

### Different Types of Web Developers

**Frontend Developer**: They handle the visible part of the website, the user interface. They use HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and frameworks like React, Vue.js, or Angular.

**Backend Developer**: They manage the server side, databases, and technical architecture. They master languages like Python, PHP, Java, Node.js.

**Full-Stack Developer**: They master both frontend and backend, making them highly sought-after profiles.

## Essential Skills to Become a Web Developer

### Technical Skills

**Fundamental Programming Languages**:
- **HTML/CSS**: The foundation of the web, essential for structuring and styling pages
- **JavaScript**: The most used language for modern web development
- **A backend language**: Python, PHP, Java, or Node.js depending on your preferences

**Frameworks and Libraries**:
- **React, Vue.js, or Angular** for frontend
- **Express, Django, Laravel** for backend
- **Git** for version control

**Databases**:
- **SQL** (MySQL, PostgreSQL) for relational databases
- **NoSQL** (MongoDB) for non-relational databases

### Essential Soft Skills

- **Problem solving**: Analyze and resolve complex bugs
- **Curiosity and continuous learning**: Technologies evolve rapidly
- **Teamwork**: Collaboration with designers, project managers
- **Rigor and attention to detail**: Clean and well-documented code

## Training to Become a Web Developer

### Traditional Academic Path

**Bac +2**:
- **BTS SIO** (IT Services for Organizations)
- **DUT Computer Science**

**Bac +3**:
- **Computer Science Degree**
- **Web Development Bachelor**

**Bac +5**:
- **Master's in Computer Science** specialized in web development
- **Engineering School** with web specialization

### Accelerated Training (Bootcamps)

**Bootcamps** are intensive 3 to 6-month training programs that allow you to quickly acquire necessary skills. They are ideal for career changes. Examples: Le Wagon, Simplon, OpenClassrooms.

**Advantages**:
- Fast and intensive training
- Concrete projects and portfolio
- Job placement support

**Disadvantages**:
- High cost (€5,000 to €10,000)
- High intensity
- Requires strong motivation

### Online Self-Training

Many platforms offer free or paid training:
- **FreeCodeCamp**: Free, very comprehensive
- **OpenClassrooms**: Certifying, with support
- **Udemy, Coursera**: On-demand courses
- **YouTube**: Free tutorials

**Tip**: Combine several resources and practice regularly on personal projects.

## Web Developer Salary in 2025

### Salaries by Experience

**Beginner (0-2 years)**:
- **€30,000 to €40,000** gross annual in France
- **€25,000 to €35,000** in Morocco

**Experienced (3-5 years)**:
- **€40,000 to €55,000** gross annual in France
- **€35,000 to €50,000** in Morocco

**Senior (5+ years)**:
- **€55,000 to €80,000** gross annual in France
- **€50,000 to €70,000** in Morocco

### Factors Influencing Salary

- **Location**: Paris and large cities offer higher salaries
- **Company type**: Startups sometimes pay less but offer more equity
- **Specialization**: Full-stack, DevOps, or rare specialties = higher salaries
- **Freelance**: Daily rates from €300 to €800 depending on experience

## Career Opportunities

### Types of Companies Recruiting

- **Web agencies**: Varied projects for different clients
- **Startups**: Dynamic environment, versatility required
- **Large corporations**: Stability, large-scale projects
- **Freelance**: Freedom, but requires finding clients

### Career Evolution

**Junior Developer** → **Experienced Developer** → **Senior Developer** → **Lead Developer** → **CTO**

Possibility to specialize: **DevOps**, **Technical Architect**, **Security Expert**, or create your own company.

## How to Get Started: Practical Action Plan

### Step 1: Choose Your Specialization

Determine if you prefer **frontend** (creativity, design) or **backend** (logic, architecture). You can also aim for **full-stack** for more versatility.

### Step 2: Acquire the Basics

Start with **HTML, CSS, and JavaScript**. Practice daily on simple projects: personal website, calculator, todo list.

### Step 3: Build a Portfolio

Create a **GitHub portfolio** with your projects. It's your best business card. Include:
- Varied projects (showcase site, application, API)
- Clean and commented code
- Detailed READMEs

### Step 4: Gain First Experience

- **Internships**: Even unpaid, they offer first experience
- **Volunteer projects**: For associations or NGOs
- **Open source contributions**: Participate in GitHub projects

### Step 5: Prepare Your Application

- **Technical CV**: Highlight your skills and projects
- **Cover letter**: Adapted to each company
- **Interview preparation**: Practice technical tests

## Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Learning too many technologies at once**: Master the basics before moving to frameworks
2. **Neglecting practice**: Theory alone is not enough, code regularly
3. **Copy-pasting without understanding**: Analyze each line of code
4. **Ignoring best practices**: Clean code, comments, tests
5. **Not staying updated**: Technologies evolve, keep learning

## FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the best language to start with?**

JavaScript is the most versatile choice as it allows both frontend and backend development (with Node.js). HTML and CSS are also essential to start.

**Do you need a degree to become a web developer?**

No, the sector prioritizes skills and portfolio. However, a degree can facilitate access to certain companies and offer better starting compensation.

**How long to become a web developer?**

With intensive training (bootcamp), count 3-6 months for the basics. For solid mastery, plan 1-2 years of regular practice.

**Can you become a web developer as self-taught?**
