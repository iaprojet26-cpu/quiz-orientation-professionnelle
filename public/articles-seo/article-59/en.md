# Business Creation Support: From Unemployed to Entrepreneur in 2026

You're **unemployed** and want to **start your business**? This is the perfect time! Many **financial aids** and support exist for job seekers who want to become entrepreneurs. In this complete guide, discover all available aids and steps to succeed in your project.

## Why Start a Business When Unemployed?

### Advantages of Business Creation While Unemployed

**1. Specific Financial Aids**: ACRE (social charges exemption), ARCE (capital from unemployment benefits), Partial maintenance of benefits, Regional and local aids

**2. Available Time**: Time to develop your project, Availability for training, Flexibility for administrative procedures

**3. Enhanced Support**: Employment agency offers support, Business creation structures (BGE, CCI, etc.), Entrepreneurship training funded

**4. Safety Net**: Possibility to combine benefits and activity income, Possible return to unemployment if failure, Less financial pressure than employee resignation

## Main Aids for Starting a Business While Unemployed

### ACRE (Aid for Business Creators and Buyers)

**What is it?** Partial exemption from social charges during the first year of activity.

**For whom?** Unemployed (compensated or not), RSA, ASS, AAH beneficiaries, Young people 18-25, Creators in priority neighborhoods

**Advantages**: 50% exemption from social charges in year 1, Savings of several thousand euros, Applicable to all legal forms

### ARCE (Aid for Business Takeover or Creation)

**What is it?** Payment of 60% of your remaining unemployment rights as capital.

**For whom?** Compensated job seekers, Business creators or buyers, ACRE beneficiaries

**Advantages**: Significant startup capital, Paid in 2 installments (50% at creation, 50% after 6 months), No repayment if failure

**Example**: If you have $20,000 in remaining unemployment rights, you receive $12,000 (60%) in capital for your business.

### Maintaining Unemployment Benefits (ARE)

**What is it?** Partial combination of your unemployment benefits with your activity income.

**Advantages**: Financial security during launch, Possible combination as long as you have rights, No time limit (within your rights limit)

**Calculation**: Benefits paid = ARE - 70% of your activity income

## ARCE or Maintaining Benefits: What to Choose?

### Comparison ARCE vs Maintaining ARE

| Criterion | ARCE | Maintaining ARE |
|-----------|------|-----------------|
| Payment | Capital in 2 installments | Monthly, according to income |
| Total Amount | 60% of rights | Up to 100% of rights |
| Security | Acquired capital | Depends on income |
| Flexibility | Irrevocable choice | Adjustable each month |

**Choose ARCE if**: You need significant startup capital, Your activity requires investments, You're confident in quick success

**Choose Maintaining ARE if**: Your activity generates progressive income, You prefer monthly income security, You don't need significant capital

## Steps to Start Your Business While Unemployed

### Step 1: Validate Your Project
- Define your business idea
- Do market research
- Test your concept (MVP, surveys)
- Take our [**orientation test**](/)

### Step 2: Develop Your Business Plan
- Project and team presentation
- Market research
- Commercial strategy
- Financial plan (3-year forecast)
- Funding needs

### Step 3: Choose Your Legal Status
**Options**: Micro-enterprise (simple and fast), EURL/SARL (limited liability), SAS/SASU (flexibility)

### Step 4: Request Aids
**ACRE Steps**: Create your business, Request ACRE (automatic for micro-enterprise), Receive exemption certificate

**ARCE or Maintaining ARE Steps**: Inform Employment agency of your project, Obtain ACRE, Choose between ARCE and maintaining ARE, Make your request

### Step 5: Create Your Business
- Registration at RCS or RNE
- Activity declaration
- Open professional bank account
- Subscribe to insurance (RC Pro, etc.)

### Step 6: Launch and Develop Your Activity
- Prospecting and first clients
- Set up tools (invoicing, accounting)
- Communication and visibility
- Monitor your cash flow

## Available Support

### Support Structures
- **Employment Agency**: Business creation workshops, Personalized support, Training funding
- **BGE**: Free support, Entrepreneurship training, Business plan help
- **CCI and CMA**: Training and workshops, Creation formalities, Post-creation support
- **Incubators**: Test your activity in real conditions, Legal hosting, Intensive support

## Tips for Success

### 1. Validate Your Idea Before Starting
Test your concept, Interview potential clients, Analyze competition

### 2. Surround Yourself
Join entrepreneur communities, Find a mentor, Participate in events

### Step 3: Master Your Finances
Separate personal and professional accounts, Monitor cash flow weekly, Anticipate charges

### 4. Be Patient and Persistent
Profitability takes time (6-18 months), First months are difficult, Perseverance is key
