# How to Improve Your Employability in 2026: 15 Effective Strategies

**Employability** is your ability to obtain and retain a job, as well as to evolve in your career. In 2026, with a constantly evolving job market, improving your employability is essential to stay competitive. In this practical guide, we present **15 effective strategies** to boost your employability.

## What is Employability in 2026?

Employability is no longer limited to having the right technical skills. In 2026, it encompasses:

- **Technical skills**: Mastery of tools and technologies
- **Soft skills**: Communication, adaptability, leadership
- **Professional network**: Relationships and visibility in your sector
- **Continuous training**: Ability to learn and adapt
- **Digital profile**: Online presence and e-reputation
- **Practical experience**: Projects and concrete achievements

Before improving your employability, **identify your professional profile**. Our [**free career orientation test**](/) helps you better know yourself and identify your strengths.

## The 15 Strategies to Improve Employability

### 1. Develop Technical Skills

**Why it's important**: Technical skills are the foundation of your employability.

**How to do it**:
- Identify skills sought in your sector
- Follow training (MOOC, certifications, bootcamps)
- Practice regularly on concrete projects
- Stay updated with new technologies

### 2. Strengthen Soft Skills

**Why it's important**: 70% of recruiters consider soft skills as important as technical skills.

**Soft skills to develop**:
- Communication and presentation
- Leadership and team management
- Problem solving
- Adaptability and resilience
- Teamwork and collaboration

### 3. Build and Maintain Professional Network

**Why it's important**: 70% of jobs are found through network.

**How to do it**:
- Join professional associations
- Participate in sector events (conferences, meetups)
- Create and maintain an optimized LinkedIn profile
- Regularly contact your professional contacts
- Offer your help before asking

### 4. Create and Maintain Professional Digital Profile

**Why it's important**: 90% of recruiters consult online profiles before an interview.

**Essential elements**:
- **LinkedIn**: Complete profile, SEO optimized, regular content
- **Online portfolio**: Website or platform (GitHub, Behance, etc.)
- **E-reputation**: Control what appears about you online
- **Professional content**: Articles, posts, relevant shares

**Tip**: Use our [**CV creation tool**](/cv) to structure your profile, then adapt it for LinkedIn.

### 5. Train Continuously

**Why it's important**: Skills have an increasingly short lifespan.

**Strategies**:
- **Short training**: Certifications, MOOC (3-6 months)
- **Long training**: Masters, specializations (1-2 years)
- **Self-learning**: Tutorials, personal projects, reading
- **Professional monitoring**: Follow your sector's news

### 6. Acquire Practical Experience

**Why it's important**: Practical experience demonstrates your skills concretely.

**How to do it**:
- **Personal projects**: Create a portfolio of achievements
- **Freelance**: Short missions to gain experience
- **Internships and apprenticeship**: Even in career transition, it's possible
- **Volunteering**: Contribute to projects that value your skills
- **Open source projects**: Contribute to community projects

### 7. Develop Your Specialization

**Why it's important**: Being a specialist in a specific field differentiates you.

**How to do it**:
- Identify a promising specialization area
- Deepen your knowledge in this area
- Obtain recognized certifications
- Create content (articles, conferences) on your specialty
- Become a reference in your field

### 8. Improve Online Visibility

**Why it's important**: Online visibility increases your opportunities.

**Strategies**:
- **Professional blog**: Share your expertise
- **Social networks**: LinkedIn, professional Twitter
- **Video**: YouTube, webinars, conferences
- **Podcast**: Create or participate in podcasts
- **Publications**: Articles in specialized media

### 9. Value Transferable Skills

**Why it's important**: Your skills can apply to different sectors.

**Transferable skills**:
- Project management
- Communication
- Analysis and problem solving
- Leadership
- Team management

**How to do it**:
- Identify your transferable skills
- Adapt your CV for each target sector
- Highlight these skills in your interviews
- Use our [**CV creation tool**](/cv) to structure your profile

### 10. Develop Company Culture Understanding

**Why it's important**: Understanding companies increases your integration chances.

**How to do it**:
- Read about companies in your sector
- Follow economic and sector news
- Understand company challenges
- Participate in company events (fairs, conferences)

### 11. Improve Written and Oral Communication

**Why it's important**: Communication is essential in all jobs.

**How to do it**:
- **Written**: Write regularly (articles, professional emails)
- **Oral**: Practice public speaking (Toastmasters, presentations)
- **Training**: Follow communication courses
- **Feedback**: Ask for feedback on your communication

### 12. Develop Adaptability

**Why it's important**: The world of work changes rapidly, adaptability is crucial.

**How to do it**:
- Accept changes and challenges
- Regularly step out of your comfort zone
- Constantly learn new things
- Stay open to new ideas and methods
- Develop resilience to failures

### 13. Create Professional Portfolio

**Why it's important**: A portfolio demonstrates your skills concretely.

**Elements to include**:
- Your projects and achievements
- Detailed case studies
- Client or colleague testimonials
- Certifications and training
- Statistics and results obtained

**Platforms**: Personal website, GitHub, Behance, online portfolio

### 14. Participate in Professional Events

**Why it's important**: Events allow networking and learning.

**Types of events**:
- Conferences and professional fairs
- Meetups and local events
- Webinars and online training
- Hackathons and competitions
- Workshops

**Benefits**:
- Professional meetings
- Learning new skills
- Visibility in your sector
- Job opportunities

### 15. Request Regular Feedback

**Why it's important**: Feedback helps you improve.

**How to do it**:
- Ask for feedback after each project
- Solicit mentors or coaches
- Participate in peer groups
- Use 360Â° evaluations
- Regularly analyze your strengths and weaknesses
